SECTION 0 — CORE GLOSSARY 
Precise definitions of all technical terms used in this framework 
 
TERM DEFINITION 
AMD Accumulation → Manipulation → Distribution. The three-phase market cycle. 
Accumulation = orders building (range). Manipulation = the false/fake directional 
move to trap retail. Distribution = the real directional move. 
Accumulation A range-bound period where neither buyers nor sellers dominate. Orders from both 
sides are being 'stacked'. Price does not trend — it consolidates. 
Manipulation 
(Inducement) 
A strong, momentum-driven, one-sided price move designed to trap retail traders into 
the wrong direction before the real move. Characterised by consecutive full-body 
candles. Also called 'inducement' or 'fake move'. 
Distribution (Real Move) The true directional move that follows manipulation. Identifiable by structured 
pullbacks, incremental lower highs/higher lows, and market structure continuation. 
Structure (BOS) Break of Structure. A confirmed breach of a prior significant swing high or low. Used to 
identify trend direction. A BOS in the direction of bias = continuation. A BOS against 
bias = must differentiate from FBoS. 
FBoS / FBoss False Break of Structure. A BOS that is caused exclusively by manipulation. It appears 
to confirm a trend change but is in fact a trap. Only created during the manipulation 
phase — never during distribution. 
RBoS / ARBoS Real Break of Structure. A BOS caused by the distribution phase — the genuine leg in 
the intended direction. Used to confirm bias direction. Only take RBoS as bias 
confirmation. 
Smart Money Trap (SMT) An order block that was created during the manipulation phase. It may give an initial 
reaction, but if price has already collected sufficient volume, it will be run through. 
SMTs are zones where smart-money concept traders anticipate reversals but get 
trapped. 
Order Block (OB) The last opposing-colour candle before a strong directional push. Represents 
institutional order placement. Becomes relevant when it has 'induced structure' — i.e., 
the move from that OB caused a structural break.

TERM DEFINITION 
Inducement OB An order block from which price moved to cause a break of a prior structural high or 
low. The only type of OB used for entries in this framework. 
DOL / Draw on Liquidity The target price must reach before reversing. Always in the direction of bias. Types: 
equal highs/lows, session highs/lows, previous daily/weekly highs/lows, SMT opens, 
structural swing points. 
Internal DOL A closer liquidity target within the current leg — used when price has already reached 
the primary DOL or when the range to the external DOL is too large for a direct move. 
Liquidity Sweep Price briefly trading beyond a structural high or low to trigger stop-losses and pending 
orders resting there, before reversing. This is the core of the FBoS entry trigger. 
Higher Time Frame (HTF) 
SMT 
An order block on a higher time frame (4H, daily) that price is reacting from. When 
price taps an HTF SMT, the lower time frame move from that point is considered HTF 
manipulation, not distribution. 
POI (Point of Interest) Any zone on the chart that could cause a significant price reaction — includes order 
blocks, SMTs, and liquidity clusters. 
Protected High/Low A swing point that has already induced price — i.e., caused a structural move FROM 
that level. Price is unlikely to revisit and break through a protected high/low. 
Break & Retest (BNR) The retail concept: price breaks a level then comes back to retest it. Used in this 
framework specifically to identify WHERE retail traders will enter so they can be 
trapped. 
Market Structure Shift 
(MSS) 
A change in the trend direction confirmed by RBoS. On lower time frames, MSS is 
triggered when the manipulation leg is overtaken — used as the break-even trigger. 
Power of 3 (P3) Another name for AMD applied to a full candle's price action — Open, Low 
(manipulation), High (distribution) for a bullish day, or Open, High (manipulation), Low 
(distribution) for a bearish day. 
OHLC Open, High, Low, Close — the anatomy of any candle. In this framework: for a bullish 
daily candle, the sequence should be Open → Low (manipulation) → High 
(distribution) → Close. 
 
SECTION 1 — AMD MARKET CYCLE 
The master framework governing all price movement 
 
  1.1  PHASE DEFINITIONS 
 
Phase 1 — ACCUMULATION Price consolidates in a range. No trending move. Both buy and sell orders are 
building. 
Visually: Price oscillates between visible support and resistance. 
Duration: Can last minutes (1M chart) or days (daily/weekly). 
Session mapping: Asia session is typically the accumulation phase for the day. 
Phase 2 — MANIPULATION Strong, one-sided, momentum candles moving AGAINST the intended real 
direction. 
Characteristics: consecutive full-body candles, fast move, no pullbacks. 

===== PAGE 3 =====

Purpose: Traps retail traders into the wrong direction; collects stop-losses; fills 
institutional orders. 
Creates: FBoS, SMTs, inducement highs/lows. 
Session mapping: London open (07:00–10:00 London time) is typically the 
manipulation phase. 
Phase 3 — DISTRIBUTION The REAL directional move toward the DOL. 
Characteristics: structured pullbacks (reaccumulation/reinducement), 
incremental structure, pullbacks do NOT violate recent swing lows/highs. 
Session mapping: NY session (or London continuation) is typically distribution. 
 
  1.2  WEEKLY AMD MAPPING 
 
Monday Accumulation for the week. Range-bound, no strong directional commitment. 
Tuesday Manipulation. Strong directional push AGAINST the weekly intended direction. 
Sets the weekly trap. Must take out a key structural point or mitigate a POI. 
Wednesday Reinducement. Internal structural points of Tuesday's distribution get taken out. 
Sets up additional trapped traders. 
Thursday Distribution / low or high of the week printed. Price moves toward the weekly 
DOL. 
Friday Rebalancing. Price cools and partially retraces. Not a primary trading day. 
Economic Calendar Rule Shift the weekly day assignments when high-impact news falls on a specific day. 
News can act as the daily/weekly manipulation trigger — reassign phases 
accordingly. 
 
SECTION 2 — BIAS DETERMINATION 
How to establish directional bias across time frames 
 
  2.1  BIAS RULES 
 
Definition Bias = the direction you expect price to travel to reach the current DOL. Bias is 
NOT set by trend lines or candlestick patterns alone. 
Bullish Bias Trigger 1. Price has tapped into a valid higher time frame SMT or inducement OB. 
2. A RBoS (real break of structure) toward the upside has been printed following 
manipulation. 
3. The DOL is above current price (equal highs, session highs, structural highs). 
Bearish Bias Trigger 1. Price has tapped into a valid higher time frame SMT or inducement OB from 
above. 
2. A RBoS toward the downside has been printed following manipulation. 
3. The DOL is below current price. 
Bias Invalidation Price breaks through the OB that created the bias (OB becomes SMT).

DOL is reached — bias must be re-evaluated from scratch. 
A higher time frame inducement leg is taken out in the opposing direction. 
Time Frame Hierarchy Higher time frame bias always overrides lower time frame signals. 
Daily bias → sets the trading direction for the day. 
Weekly bias → sets the context for the daily bias. 
Use 1H or 4H for structural confirmation; use M5 or M1 for entry execution. 
Bias After DOL Hit Once DOL is reached, STOP trading toward it. Do NOT extend targets 
speculatively. Re-identify the new DOL with fresh confirmation before taking any 
position. 
 
  2.2  TREND SWITCHING (How Bias Changes) 
 
Step 1 — Existing Trend A clear sequence of higher highs + higher lows (bullish) or lower highs + lower 
lows (bearish) is in place. 
Step 2 — Accumulation Price stops trending and consolidates. Volume decreases. No new highs/lows 
printed. 
Step 3 — Manipulation A strong move OPPOSITE to the previous trend occurs. This creates FBoS — 
appears to confirm a new trend but is a trap. Retail traders reverse their bias. 
Step 4 — RBoS Confirmation After manipulation, price reverses and prints a REAL break of structure in the 
ORIGINAL direction (or the new intended direction). THIS is the bias confirmation 
trigger. 
Step 5 — New Bias Active Bias flips only after RBoS. SMTs from the manipulation phase are now identified. 
DOL in the new direction is set. Trade continuation models toward new DOL. 
Key Rule You CANNOT confirm a bias change from FBoS alone. Only RBoS after 
manipulation constitutes a genuine trend switch. 
 
SECTION 3 — SMART MONEY TRAP (SMT) 
When order blocks become traps 
 
  3.1  DEFINITION & CREATION 
 
What is an SMT? An order block that was created by the manipulation phase of AMD. Retail and 
smart-money-concept traders view these as valid reversal zones, making them 
high-probability trap locations. 
How an OB becomes an SMT — 
Rule 1: DOL Reached 
When price reaches the DOL, all OBs that were previously used as continuation 
entries now convert to SMTs. 
Logic: If the primary move target is already hit, the directional bias reverses. OBs 
in the prior direction are no longer valid continuation entries. 
How an OB becomes an SMT — 
Rule 2: BOS Away 
When a strong RBoS is printed AWAY from an OB (i.e., price moves away with 
conviction and does not return), that OB is reclassified as an SMT. 

===== PAGE 5 =====

Logic: Price has demonstrated it does not need that OB for support/resistance. 
Any return will be a trap. 
How an OB becomes an SMT — 
Rule 3: FBoS Origin 
Any OB that was created during a manipulation move is immediately classified as 
an SMT. It may give a reaction (inducing traders), but it will ultimately be run 
through unless volume is still required. 
SMT vs. Valid OB Valid OB: Created by RBoS / distribution. Has not been tapped. DOL not yet 
reached. 
SMT: Created by FBoS / manipulation. OR DOL has been reached. OR strong BOS 
printed away from it. 
HTF SMT A SMT visible on 4H or daily chart. When price taps an HTF SMT, the entire lower 
time frame move from that zone is classified as HTF manipulation — even if lower 
time frame shows distribution. This justifies continuation trades from LTF OBs 
within that HTF zone. 
 
▶  SMT REACTION RULE 
When price taps an HTF SMT and shows an initial rejection, this does NOT automatically mean distribution starts 
immediately. 
Price will typically: (1) Give initial reaction from SMT → (2) Accumulate / form miniature AMD at the SMT level → (3) 
Then distribute away from SMT. 
Do NOT enter on the first candle touching an SMT. Wait for confirmation per the entry model rules in Section 6. 
 
SECTION 4 — DRAW ON LIQUIDITY (DOL) 
Target identification and management 
 
  4.1  TYPES OF DOL (in order of priority) 
 
Equal Highs / Equal Lows Two or more swing points at the same price level. Extremely visible to retail 
traders → magnets for stop-loss clusters → high probability DOL target. 
Session Highs / Lows (Asia, 
London, NY) 
The high or low printed during a specific session. If the session has NOT induced 
(i.e., no structural break FROM that level), it remains a valid DOL target. 
Previous Daily High / Low (PDH 
/ PDL) 
Prior day's high or low. Valid DOL unless already swept or if price has induced 
structure from beyond that level. 
Previous Weekly High / Low Prior week's high or low. Used for HTF bias and macro DOL targeting. 
SMT Open Price (Preferred TP 
Target) 
The opening price of an SMT candle. Preferred take-profit target within this 
framework. Reason: price frequently stalls at SMT opens before deciding 
direction. Reduces exposure and avoids holding through reversals. 
Structural Swing Point Any clear prior high/low that has obvious stop-loss clusters resting beyond it. 
Valid when other types do not apply. 
 
  4.2  DOL RULES

DOL Must Align With Bias Only target a DOL that is in the direction of your current bias. Never target a DOL 
in the counter-bias direction as a primary target. 
DOL Invalidation Criteria An equal high/low is NOT a valid DOL if: price has already induced structure from 
BEYOND that level (i.e., created a protected high/low). 
A session high/low is NOT valid if: the session has already induced (RBoS from 
that session's move in the bias direction). 
PDH/PDL is NOT valid if: already swept, or the structural context has changed. 
After DOL is Hit Immediately stop trading in the prior bias direction. Re-run top-down analysis. 
Identify new DOL. Wait for fresh manipulation and RBoS before entering again. 
Internal DOL Used when the primary DOL is far away and price needs to rebalance before 
continuing. 
Identify: nearest structural high/low in the bias direction within the current leg. 
Condition: price must have already shown signs of reaccumulation after a 
distribution leg. 
Usage: target internal DOL for first position; only extend to external DOL after 
internal is taken and structure supports it. 
 
SECTION 5 — FBoS ENTRY MODEL 
False Break of Structure — Complete Specification 
 
▶  FBoS DEFINITION 
A FBoS is a break of a prior structural high or low that was caused exclusively by the MANIPULATION phase of AMD. 
It appears to retail traders as a confirmed break of structure (a new trend), but it is a deliberate trap. 
FBoS can ONLY exist if: Accumulation preceded it, AND the breakout move is one-sided momentum candles (no 
pullbacks), AND price subsequently reverses in the intended direction. 
 
  5.1  FBoS EXACT ENTRY CRITERIA (ALL must be met) 
 
Criterion 1 — Prior Accumulation A visible range/consolidation must have formed BEFORE the FBoS move. Price 
must have been range-bound (Asia session, or visible sideways structure on 
the chart). 
Criterion 2 — Momentum 
Breakout 
The move creating the FBoS must be strong, one-sided, consecutive full-body 
candles. No significant pullbacks during the move. Partial candles or gradual 
moves do NOT qualify as manipulation. 
Criterion 3 — Structural Level 
Taken 
The FBoS must have swept through a visible structural high/low — not just 
wicked through. The candle must close beyond the level (not just wick). The 
level must have been visible and likely held stop-losses. 
Criterion 4 — Bias Alignment The anticipated real move (distribution) must align with the higher time frame 
bias. Do NOT enter FBoS setups that are counter to HTF bias. 
Criterion 5 — DOL Identified Before entry, a clear DOL in the bias direction must be identified. The DOL 
must be at least 2x the stop-loss distance away (minimum 2R target visible). 

===== PAGE 7 =====

Criterion 6 — Timing (Optional but 
Preferred) 
London session (07:00–10:00): preferred manipulation window for EU pairs. 
NY open (13:00–16:00 London time): secondary manipulation window. 
Avoid FBoS setups that appear at 4PM London close (often continuation, not 
reversal) unless explicitly confirmed by AMD structure. 
Criterion 7 — Entry Trigger Option A — Aggressive: Enter at the sweep candle's close (requires 
experience). Stop loss = 1–3 pips beyond the swept structural level. 
Option B — Conservative: Wait for a bullish/bearish confirmation candle (close 
in opposing direction) AFTER the sweep. Enter on next candle open. 
Option C — Mitigation: Wait for price to pull back to the OB formed by the 
FBoS leg. Enter when price taps the OB (see Section 6 — Mitigation Model for 
exact rules). 
 
  5.2  FBoS EXIT CRITERIA 
 
Take Profit (Primary) Opening price of the nearest SMT in the bias direction. This is the preferred TP 
level — do not extend unless volume and structure clearly support continuation. 
Take Profit (Secondary) Next significant DOL: equal highs/lows, session high/low, or previous daily 
high/low in the bias direction. 
Break Even Trigger Move stop to break even ONLY after: the pullback leg (mitigation of the FBoS 
candle) has been overtaken by price moving in the trade direction. 
Rule: Do NOT go break even during the first pullback. Wait for the pullback's high 
(for shorts) or low (for longs) to be taken out by the next impulse leg. 
Reason: Premature break even during mitigation pullback results in being 
stopped out before the real distribution begins. 
Partial Exit Rule If trading multiple positions: close 50% at first DOL (internal), leave 50% running 
to secondary DOL. Move remainder to break even after partial close. 
 
  5.3  FBoS INVALIDATION CONDITIONS 
 
▶  INVALIDATION — TRADE IS VOID IF ANY APPLY 
1. Price re-enters accumulation range after the FBoS move (structure has not committed to direction). 
2. The manipulation move has pullbacks — it is not one-sided momentum. This indicates distribution, not 
manipulation. 
3. HTF SMT or OB is located within the stop-loss distance in the trade direction (price may be reacting from HTF 
structure, not distributing). 
4. DOL has already been reached on this session/day — do not enter FBoS setups post-DOL. 
5. Economic calendar: high-impact news is imminent within 15 minutes of entry — avoid entries immediately before 
major news events. 
6. The swept structural level is a 'protected high/low' (already induced structure previously) — not a valid liquidity 
target.

Trend Requirement FBoS works in BOTH trending and ranging markets. In a trending market, FBoS in 
the trend direction is higher probability. Counter-trend FBoS requires HTF SMT 
confluence. 
Liquidity Condition High-probability: Visible equal highs/lows were present before the FBoS (obvious

SECTION 6 — MITIGATION ENTRY MODEL 
Order Block Mitigation — Complete Specification 
 
▶  MITIGATION DEFINITION 
Mitigation is an entry model where price returns to tap into an Order Block (OB) that caused a structural break 
(inducement OB), after that OB's move has already been validated. 
Unlike FBoS (which enters at the sweep), Mitigation enters at the origin zone of the prior impulsive move. 
This model provides a higher Risk:Reward entry at the cost of a lower fill probability — price may not always return 
to mitigate. 
 
  6.1  IDENTIFYING A VALID INDUCEMENT ORDER BLOCK 
 
OB Candle Definition The last opposing-colour candle BEFORE a strong directional push. 
For a bullish OB: the last bearish (red/down-close) candle before a strong 
bullish move. 
For a bearish OB: the last bullish (green/up-close) candle before a strong 
bearish move. 
OB Zone Boundaries Top: highest point of the OB candle (wick high for bearish OB / body high for 
aggressive marking). 
Bottom: lowest point of the OB candle (wick low for bullish OB / body low for 
aggressive marking). 
Entry preference: Use the WICK extremes for maximum zone coverage. For 
tighter entries with experience: use the candle body only. 
Inducement Condition 
(Mandatory) 
The OB must have caused a break of a prior structural high or low. 
Process: OB candle forms → price pushes from OB → prior structural swing 
high/low is taken → this confirms the OB 'induced' structure. 
An OB without inducement is NOT a valid mitigation entry — it has no 
institutional significance. 

===== PAGE 9 =====

OB Candle Selection Use the candle that swept liquidity (wicked beyond a prior level) as the OB, 
NOT just any opposing candle. 
The OB candle that swept the most liquidity (largest wick) has the highest 
probability of being respected on mitigation. 
 
  6.2  MITIGATION EXACT ENTRY CRITERIA (ALL must be met) 
 
Criterion 1 — Valid Inducement OB 
Identified 
The OB has already induced structure (caused a structural break). The OB 
zone has been clearly marked with top and bottom. 
Criterion 2 — OB Not Previously 
Tapped 
The OB zone has NOT been visited since it was created. A previously tapped 
OB loses validity — it becomes an SMT or a weaker zone. 
Criterion 3 — DOL Clearly Identified A DOL beyond the OB (in the trade direction) must be visible. Minimum R:R 
of 2:1 required from entry to DOL. 
Criterion 4 — Bias Alignment HTF bias must support the mitigation entry direction. Do not take bullish 
mitigation entries in a bearish HTF environment unless in a confirmed 
counter-bias pullback setup with explicit HTF SMT confluence. 
Criterion 5 — Entry Trigger 
(Conservative) 
Wait for price to TAP into the OB zone (touch the zone boundary). 
THEN wait for a M5 BOS (break of structure on M5) in the trade direction 
after the tap. 
Enter on the next candle open following the M5 BOS. 
Stop loss: below the OB wick extreme (for bullish OB) or above the OB wick 
extreme (for bearish OB). 
Criterion 6 — Entry Trigger 
(Aggressive / With Experience Only) 
Enter limit order at the OB zone boundary before price arrives. 
Stop loss: same as above. 
Use ONLY if: the OB has extreme confluence (HTF SMT + inducement + 
timing all align). 
Criterion 7 — Timing Alignment OB should be tapped during London (07:00–13:00) or NY (13:00–17:00) 
sessions. 
OB tapped during Asia session = lower probability unless pair is Asia-active 
(JPY, CHF pairs). 
 
  6.3  MITIGATION EXIT CRITERIA 
 
Take Profit (Primary) SMT open price of the nearest SMT in the bias direction. This is the default TP. 
Conservative and consistent with the framework's risk management philosophy. 
Take Profit (Secondary) Next clear DOL: equal highs/lows, session high/low, previous daily high/low. Only 
extend to secondary TP if price has shown clear continuation momentum past the 
primary TP level WITHOUT closing a full opposing candle. 
Break Even Trigger Move to break even ONLY after: price takes out the most recent internal swing 
high (for longs) or swing low (for shorts) that formed AFTER entry. 
Rule: The first pullback after entry is often a miniature AMD — do NOT go break 
even during this pullback. 
Trigger: When the high of the first post-entry pullback is taken out by price (for 
longs), go break even.

Stop Loss Placement Place stop loss BELOW the wick low of the OB candle (for bullish mitigation) or 
ABOVE the wick high (for bearish mitigation). 
Do NOT place stop loss at the OB body boundary — the wick will frequently be 
swept before reversal. 
If the OB has an extreme wick (10+ pips), consider placing stop 1–2 pips beyond 
the wick for acceptable R:R. 
 
  6.4  MITIGATION INVALIDATION CONDITIONS 
 
▶  INVALIDATION — TRADE IS VOID IF ANY APPLY 
1. OB has already been tapped once since it was created — it is now either an SMT or a depleted zone. 
2. A BOS in the opposing direction has occurred on M15 or higher since the OB was created — the OB may now be 
acting as an SMT. 
3. Price has already reached the primary DOL this session — do not enter mitigation setups post-DOL. 
4. The OB is located WITHIN the accumulation range (not at the edge of the range) — internal OBs are SMTs unless 
confirmed by HTF structure. 
5. The inducement from the OB was not 'sharp' — i.e., a gradual move rather than a momentum impulse. Only 
momentum-based inducements create valid OBs. 
6. HTF context has changed: if a higher-time-frame OB or SMT is located within the stop-loss distance, the trade is 
invalidated. 
 
  6.5  OB TO SMT TRANSITION SUMMARY 
 
Trigger 1 — DOL Hit OB → SMT as soon as the primary DOL in the direction of the OB's bias has been 
reached. All OBs that were used for continuation now become traps. 
Trigger 2 — Strong BOS Away OB → SMT when a strong, momentum RBoS is printed AWAY from the OB and 
price shows no intention of returning within a reasonable time. The OB is 
abandoned by the market. 
Trigger 3 — FBoS Origin Any OB created during a manipulation leg is classified as SMT from its creation. 
May give initial reaction (traders pile in), but will ultimately be violated if the real 
move is strong. 
Trigger 4 — Range Too 
Extended 
If price has moved significantly in the opposing direction from the OB (e.g., 100+ 
pips away on EU), the OB is no longer acting as a relevant mitigation target — it 
becomes an extreme SMT for a counter-bias reversal setup only.

SECTION 9 — STOP LOSS & BREAK EVEN PROTOCOL 
 
Stop Loss Placement Rule FBoS: 1–3 pips beyond the swept structural level (wick extreme). 
Mitigation: Beyond the OB candle's wick extreme in the opposing direction. 
NEVER place SL at the OB body boundary — wicks will sweep it. 
Break Even Rule DO NOT go break even on the first post-entry pullback. 
GO break even when: the high of the pullback leg (for longs) OR the low of the 
pullback leg (for shorts) is taken out by the next impulse candle. 
Rationale: First pullback is often miniature AMD. Going BE too early = stopped 
out before distribution begins. 
Protected Lows/Highs Rule If the post-entry pullback prints a new swing low (for longs) that is LOWER than 
the entry-zone swing low, the setup is weakening. Reassess but do NOT 
immediately exit — wait for a clear BOS violation. 
Maximum Loss Rule If the OB wick extreme is violated (price closes beyond SL), the trade is fully 
invalidated. Exit immediately. Do not average down or move stop.

SECTION 10 — QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST 
 
  Pre-Trade Checklist (run in order) 
 
1.  Identify HTF bias (daily/weekly). Confirm via RBoS after manipulation. Note DOL. 
2.  Map the current AMD phase. Is price in accumulation, manipulation, or distribution? 
3.  Identify session timing. London open? NY open? 4PM close? 
4.  Confirm DOL is not yet reached. If reached — DO NOT trade in that direction. 
5.  Mark all inducement OBs on M5 and M15. Classify each as valid OB or SMT. 
6.  Identify retail traps visible: trend lines broken? BNR zones formed? Equal highs/lows swept? 
7.  Select entry model (FBoS or Mitigation for beginners). 
8.  Confirm entry trigger (rejection candle / M5 BOS) is present. 
9.  Set stop loss at OB wick extreme. Confirm minimum 2R to nearest DOL. 
10. Set take profit at SMT open price (primary) or next DOL (secondary). 
11. Set break-even rule BEFORE entry: 'I go BE when [specific swing level] is taken.' 
12. Check economic calendar — no high-impact news within 15 minutes of entry. 
 
▶  CORE PRINCIPLE 
The market is driven by the need to trap the 95% (retail). Every setup in this framework exploits a moment where 
retail traders are collectively positioned in the wrong direction. 
Your edge is: identifying WHERE retail is trapped → entering OPPOSITE to their position → targeting WHERE their 
stop losses are. 
If you cannot clearly articulate which retail traders are trapped and where their stops are — do not take the trade.
